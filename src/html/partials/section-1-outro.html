Florida particularly at risk

With an abundant, almost year-round population of Aedes aegypti mosquitoes (the bloodsuckers capable of transmitting the illness) and a high volume of travelers from Zika-prone countries, the Sunshine State is especially vulnerable to an outbreak. In February 2016, the CDC began to issue weekly counts of cases reported by states and territories of laboratory-confirmed symptomatic cases. In February 2017 they began reporting blood donors whose blood tested positive for Zika virus RNA regardless of being symptomatic.
